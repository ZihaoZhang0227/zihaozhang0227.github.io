1. Spend two minutes with the experience and list all of your actions in granular detail.

a) When I first opened the website, I found a black guide interface. This guide interface made mefeel like a curtain, slowly pulling the page away.

b) Then came a series of carousels that introduced the recent activities of NGV.

c) Here I noticed the upper navigation bar of NGV, which shows the most basic information about entering NGV, such as how to buy tickets, etc.

d) I started to enlarge the page to full screen, and I found that NGV's website adopts a relatively basic page layout.

e) I started to scroll down the page and it felt good to me. Each story displayed is divided into small areas, and with a gradient effect, it feels very soft.

f) Swipe to the bottom, and there are many humanized consultation questions.

2. What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

In the whole experience, the first thing that caught my eye was the carousel image when the page was opened. The images that are constantly cross converted here are images with higher saturation, which makes it easy for the audience to see the information at a glance. This is also what you need to do to make a web page. Try to let the audience see what they want to know at first glance. If it is too complicated, it will consume users' money.

3. What did you spend the most time engaging with?

While viewing the entire page, I spent most of my time reading the introduction to each work and appreciating the artist's work.

4.What was the most common action in your two-minute interaction with the experience?

In the process of experience, what I use most often is to swipe down the page and click the find out more button to view more artists' works and introductions.

5.What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

As an audience, I can open the page to find out what exhibitions NGV is currently holding, and I can intuitively understand the content of some exhibitions on the official website of NGV.

6.How does the experience communicate it's primary goal?

On the official website of NGV, there are many buttons to click. When I want to know more stories about Picasso, I can continue to click and browse. In addition, there are many expanded functions, such as how to buy tickets, view addresses, and more.

In addition to looking up more Picasso information, I interact with the browser about once every 30 seconds. A quick preview of the general information, if I am interested, I will continue to click on the details to continue to learn more.

10.What is the most frustrating element of the interaction and what makes it frustrating to you?

The multilingual system of this site is very disappointing. The English version of the website not only has the introduction of the event, but also the introduction of the artist, rich in color matching. However, only the English-only part of the site is very interactive, and other languages have only a few thin pages. When I browsed the site in Japanese, I found that only the first page was in Japanese, and all the secondary pages jumped back to English.

11.What is the most satisfying element of the interaction and what makes it satisfying to you?

What I like most about this page is that there is so much useful information in it. This mainly depends on the effective layout of the page. There will not be too many words to consume the patience of users. In addition, the effective layout and the side-by-side arrangement of pictures and words are all I can learn from.
